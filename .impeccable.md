## Design Context

### Users

Minance serves privacy-conscious people who self-host their personal-finance workspace. They use it to import and review transactions, understand spending and income, maintain categories and recurring activity, and make confident decisions without handing their financial history to a hosted service.

### Brand Personality

Simple, trustworthy, sophisticated. The interface should feel calm and assured: precise enough for financial decisions, approachable enough for everyday use, and transparent about what it will change.

### Aesthetic Direction

Emulate the clarity and polish of Copilot Money: beautifully organized financial information, strong hierarchy, compact native-feeling controls, and automation that removes routine work. Preserve Minance's editorial typography, restrained green accent, privacy-first self-host identity, and equally complete dark and light themes. Avoid dashboard clutter, ornamental cards, accounting-heavy jargon, and interactions that conceal consequential changes.

### Design Principles

1. Make the current financial state immediately legible; details should be available through progressive disclosure.
2. Prefer smart, reversible defaults and automatic application when the user's intent is unambiguous.
3. Keep actions compact and contextual, while retaining explicit labels, tooltips, focus states, and keyboard access.
4. Use color and decoration sparingly so that financial meaning and hierarchy carry the interface.
5. Treat trust as a product feature: communicate scope clearly, preserve user control, and never imply that data left the self-hosted workspace.
